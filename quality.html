The Process

Quality Control Stages

We implement a robust three-stage inspection process to catch and correct issues early, preventing costly delays and ensuring perfect final products.

🧵

Pre-Production Inspection (PPI)

Before manufacturing begins, we inspect all raw materials. We verify yarn quality, fabric GSM, color lab dips, accessories, and packaging materials against approved samples.

⚙️

Inline Inspection (DUPRO)

During Production (DUPRO) inspections occur when 20-30% of the order is completed. We check cutting precision, stitching quality, and identify any deviations from specifications.

📦

Final Random Inspection (FRI)

Conducted when 100% of goods are manufactured and at least 80% packed. We use AQL sampling to verify workmanship, dimensions, labeling, and packing integrity before authorizing shipment.

Testing & Certifications

Rigorous Testing Standards

📜

ISO Standards

Manufacturing partners comply with ISO 9001 Quality Management standards.

📊

AQL Inspection

We utilize ANSI/ASQ Z1.4 sampling plans for all shipment inspections.

🧶

Fabric Testing

Testing for tensile strength, tear resistance, pilling, and GSM accuracy.

🎨

Color Fastness

Rigorous testing for color fastness to washing, light, and rubbing (crocking).

📏

Shrinkage Testing

Pre and post-wash dimensional stability testing to ensure correct sizing.

🧲

Needle Detection

All garments and baby textiles pass through industrial metal detectors.

✨

Visual Inspection

Detailed checking for sewing defects, loose threads, stains, and symmetry.

🏷️

Packing & Marking

Verification of barcodes, care labels, carton strength, and shipping marks.

AQL Standards

Acceptable Quality Limits We Follow

We typically follow AQL 2.5 for Major defects and AQL 4.0 for Minor defects, though stricter standards can be applied based on your specific requirements.

Defect Classification Description Typical AQL Level
Critical Defects Defects that could cause harm to the user or fail to comply with regulations (e.g., broken needles, sharp edges, missing warning labels). 0.0 (Zero Tolerance)
Major Defects Defects that affect the usability or saleability of the product, causing the end user to return it (e.g., open seams, incorrect sizing, obvious stains, fabric holes). 1.5 / 2.5
Minor Defects Defects that represent a slight departure from specifications but do not affect the product's usability (e.g., untrimmed threads, slight uneven stitching, minor shade variation). 4.0

* Full inspection reports with high-resolution photography are provided to clients prior to container loading.
